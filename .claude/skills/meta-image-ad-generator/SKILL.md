# PART 1: META AD FUNDAMENTALS

## Platform Reality
Meta (Facebook + Instagram) is:
- **Interruption advertising**: Nobody came to see your ad
- **Scroll-based**: 1-2 seconds to earn attention
- **Intent-free**: Unlike search, no expressed intent
- **Visual-first**: Image/video is processed before text
- **Algorithm-fed**: Creative quality determines distribution

**Implication**: Every element must earn its right to exist.

## The Ad Hierarchy
```
1. Creative (image/video) — stops the scroll
2. Headline — delivers the core message
3. Primary text — develops interest and desire
4. Description — adds specificity or urgency
5. CTA button — signals the action
```

**Most impact**: Creative > Headline > Primary text first line

## What Actually Stops the Scroll
- **Faces**: Especially direct eye contact
- **Movement** (in static ads): Implied or suggested movement
- **Colour contrast**: Against Facebook's blue/white
- **Text on image**: Short, high-contrast, readable
- **Unexpected elements**: Something that doesn't "look like an ad"
- **Pattern disruption**: Breaks visual expectations for the context

# PART 2: THE COLD TRAFFIC PROBLEM

## The Awareness Ladder (Applied to Meta)

**Problem-Unaware Traffic** (hardest — most Facebook traffic):
→ Lead with story or surprising fact
→ Don't mention the product upfront
→ Make them feel a problem they're not yet labelling

**Problem-Aware Traffic** (common for targeted audiences):
→ Name their pain directly in headline or image
→ Quick empathy then solution
→ More direct, less story needed

**Solution-Aware Traffic** (warm audiences, retargeting):
→ Lead with mechanism or proof
→ Why your solution beats alternatives
→ Can be more direct about product

**Product-Aware Traffic** (hottest — retargeting, lookalikes):
→ Offer-led creative
→ Price, terms, guarantees prominent
→ Urgency and scarcity appropriate here

# PART 3: CREATIVE CONCEPT FRAMEWORK

## The 8 Meta Ad Concept Types

### 1. The Testimonial Ad
**Format**: Real customer quote, ideally with their photo
**Psychology**: Social proof, relatability
**Best for**: Middle to bottom of funnel, overcoming objections

```
Image concept: Photo of customer + quote overlay
Headline: [The specific result from the testimonial]
Primary text: [Their story expanded] + [Offer + CTA]
```

### 2. The Problem-Agitation Ad
**Format**: Identifies a specific pain point viscerally
**Psychology**: Recognition, "finally someone gets it"
**Best for**: Cold audiences, problem-aware

```
Image concept: Visual representation of the problem/frustration
Headline: [Name the problem directly]
Primary text: [Agitate → empathise → hint at solution] + [CTA]
```

### 3. The Results Ad
**Format**: Before/after, screenshot of results, numbers
**Psychology**: Proof, aspiration, credibility
**Best for**: Warm traffic, skeptical audiences

```
Image concept: Before/after split OR screenshot OR data visual
Headline: [The specific result achieved]
Primary text: [How they got there] + [That's what [product] does] + [CTA]
```

### 4. The Curiosity/Intrigue Ad
**Format**: Incomplete information that demands completion
**Psychology**: Zeigarnik effect, open loops
**Best for**: Cold traffic, high-awareness saturation markets

```
Image concept: Intriguing visual with question or incomplete statement
Headline: [The question or incomplete statement]
Primary text: [Begin answering, don't fully resolve] + [CTA to get full answer]
```

### 5. The Authority Ad
**Format**: Expert/founder/professional + credentials
**Psychology**: Trust, expertise, authority transfer
**Best for**: High-consideration purchases, B2B, professional services

```
Image concept: Professional photo + credential overlay
Headline: [The bold claim from the authority]
Primary text: [The insight/solution] + [Who this is from] + [CTA]
```

### 6. The Offer Ad
**Format**: Clear, prominent offer with price/value
**Psychology**: Loss aversion, value recognition
**Best for**: Warm traffic, retargeting, promotion periods

```
Image concept: Product + offer overlay (price, discount, bonus)
Headline: [The offer in bold]
Primary text: [What's included + why it's valuable + urgency] + [CTA]
```

### 7. The Story/Native Ad
**Format**: Looks like organic content — story or insight post
**Psychology**: Trust (doesn't feel like ad), engagement
**Best for**: Cold traffic, content-saturated audiences

```
Image concept: Lifestyle/real photo, no design/branding
Headline: [Teaser that continues the story]
Primary text: [Full story or insight — no hard sell upfront] + [Soft CTA at end]
```

### 8. The Video Concept (Image Adaptation)
**Format**: Static image that looks like a video thumbnail
**Psychology**: Curiosity (what's in the video?), pattern interrupt
**Best for**: Driving to video content, webinars, VSLs

```
Image concept: Screenshot-style image with play button overlay
Headline: [What they'll learn from the "video"]
Primary text: [The insight preview] + [CTA to watch]
```

# PART 4: COPY FRAMEWORKS

## Primary Text Frameworks

### Framework 1: The PAS Hook
```
[Problem — name it immediately]
[Agitate — make it feel real and specific]
[Solution — introduce the fix]
[CTA]

Example:
"Still struggling with [problem]?
You've tried [common solution A] and [common solution B].
Neither worked. Here's why — and what does.
[Learn more — link]"
```

### Framework 2: The Social Proof Hook
```
[Result achieved by a real person]
[Who they are (relatable)]
[How they did it]
[You can too / CTA]

Example:
"[Name] [achieved result] in [timeframe].
She's a [relatable descriptor].
Here's exactly what she did differently.
[See her story — link]"
```

### Framework 3: The Question Hook
```
[Question that creates identification]
[If yes: here's what that means]
[Here's what to do about it]
[CTA]

Example:
"Are you [experiencing specific situation]?
If so, it's not [obvious cause].
It's [real cause — creating curiosity].
[Find out what to do — link]"
```

### Framework 4: The Bold Claim Hook
```
[Big claim — leading with the result]
[The proof or credibility for that claim]
[How]
[CTA]

Example:
"[Impressive result] in [timeframe].
[X people/companies] have done it.
No [sacrifice/objection] required.
[Here's how — link]"
```

### Framework 5: The Story Open
```
[Specific moment or scene]
[The conflict]
[The turn]
[The offer]

Example:
"[Specific time/place], [relatable person] was [struggling with specific problem].
[They tried X. They tried Y.]
Then [they discovered Z].
Now [they have the result].
[CTA to learn about Z]"
```

# PART 5: HEADLINE FORMULAS

## The Meta Ad Headline (The 40-Character Challenge)
In Meta ads, headlines are short. Maximum 40 characters visible without truncation.

### Formula Types

**Benefit Headlines** (most reliable):
- "[Specific Result] — [How]"
- "Get [Result] Without [Sacrifice]"
- "[Result] For [Target Audience]"

**Curiosity Headlines**:
- "The [Number]-Step [Method]"
- "Why [Thing] Doesn't Work"
- "[Number] [People] Can't Be Wrong"

**Offer Headlines**:
- "[Discount/Offer]: Ends [When]"
- "Free [Thing] For [Qualifier]"
- "From $[Price] — [What You Get]"

**Social Proof Headlines**:
- "[Number] [Happy Customers]"
- "As Seen In [Publication]"
- "[Impressive Statistic] Achieved"

**Direct Question Headlines**:
- "Ready For [Result]?"
- "Still [Experiencing Problem]?"
- "Want [Specific Outcome]?"

# PART 6: AD SET STRUCTURE

## Minimum Viable Ad Set
For any campaign, create minimum 3 creative variants:

**Variant A**: Testimonial/social proof angle
**Variant B**: Problem/agitation angle
**Variant C**: Results/transformation angle

## Full Test Ad Set (3x3 Structure)
Test 3 hooks × 3 angles = 9 variants

**3 Hooks to test**:
1. Problem-led
2. Curiosity-led
3. Social proof-led

**3 Angles to test**:
1. Primary pain point
2. Secondary pain point / alternative desire
3. Objection handler ("even if you've tried everything")

# PART 7: PLATFORM COMPLIANCE

## Facebook Ad Policy Summary

### Hard No's
- Before/after images for health products
- Income claims without substantial disclaimer
- References to personal attributes ("You are overweight")
- Shocking or sensational content
- Misleading or false claims
- Adult content (unless specifically approved account)

### Gray Areas (Proceed With Caution)
- Health claims (need substantiation)
- Financial claims (need disclaimer)
- Testimonials with specific results (add "results may vary")
- Direct social pressure ("Your friends are already...")

### Image Guidelines
- Avoid excessive text on image (less is more)
- No graphic/disturbing content
- Models must represent the product being sold
- No prohibited imagery (weapons, explicit content)

## Safe Claim Formats
```
Instead of: "Lose 10kg in 30 days"
Use: "Many of our customers report significant results within 30 days"

Instead of: "Make $5,000 this month"
Use: "Here's how [Name] generated $5,000 in his first month [with disclosure]"

Instead of: "Cure your [condition]"
Use: "Support your [health goal] naturally"
```
