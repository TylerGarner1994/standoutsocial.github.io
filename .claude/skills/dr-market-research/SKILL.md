# DR Market Research Skill

## Activation
Use this skill when: Conducting deep market research, understanding customer psychology, competitive landscape analysis.

**Activate with**: "Run DR market research", "Deep market research", "Understand the market"

## Research Framework

### Phase 1: Market Definition
**Questions to answer**:
- What exact problem does this product/service solve?
- Who has this problem acutely enough to pay for a solution?
- How big is this population?
- What's the current behaviour (what are they doing about it now)?

**Output**: Market definition statement
```
"[Product/service] helps [specific demographic] who [experience specific problem] to [achieve specific outcome] even if [common objection/limitation]."
```

### Phase 2: Customer Psychology Deep Dive

**The Pain Stack**
For any problem, map the layers:
1. **Surface pain**: What they say the problem is
2. **Underlying pain**: What's really bothering them
3. **Core emotional pain**: The identity/status/relationship dimension
4. **Fear**: What they're afraid of if this continues
5. **Shame**: What they won't admit about this problem

**The Desire Stack**
1. **Surface desire**: What they say they want
2. **Underlying desire**: What they really want
3. **Identity desire**: Who they want to be
4. **Dream outcome**: The ultimate vision

**The Objection Stack**
1. **Rational objections** (time, money, complexity)
2. **Emotional objections** (fear of failure, trust issues)
3. **Experience objections** ("I've tried this before")
4. **Belief objections** ("I don't think I can do this")

### Phase 4: Competitive Landscape
**Direct competitors**: Same product/service category
**Indirect competitors**: Different approach, same problem
**Status quo**: What people currently do instead

For each competitor:
- Core positioning claim
- Price point
- Proof strategy
- Audience focus
- Perceived weakness

### Phase 5: Market Intelligence Synthesis

**Opportunity Identification**:
- What pain is NOT being addressed?
- What segment is UNDERSERVED?
- What message is NOT being told?
- What proof TYPE is nobody using?

**Message Hierarchy**:
```
Primary message: [The single most important thing to communicate]
Secondary message: [The supporting claim that builds on primary]
Tertiary message: [The objection handler]
```

**Positioning Statement**:
```
For [target customer] who [has this need/problem],
[Product/brand] is [category/reference frame]
that [key differentiator/benefit].
Unlike [alternatives], [product] [key differentiation].
```

## Quick Research (30 Minutes)
When time is limited:
1. Google the product category + "review" — read top reviews
2. Find the top 3 Reddit threads on the problem
3. Check Amazon reviews for competing products (3-star especially)
4. Note: What words do they use? What's their biggest frustration?
5. Identify: What one thing would unlock the most conversions?
