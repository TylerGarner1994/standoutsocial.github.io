# Competitive Intelligence Skill

## Activation
Use this skill when: Analysing competitor positioning, messaging, offers, and strategy to inform creative and strategic decisions.

**Activate with**: "Run competitive intelligence", "Analyse competitors", "What are competitors doing?"

## Intelligence Framework

### What To Collect
For each competitor:

**1. Positioning**
- Headline/tagline on homepage
- Core value proposition
- Who they're targeting (explicit + implied)
- What category do they claim to own?

**2. Offer Structure**
- Price points (if visible)
- Packaging/tiers
- Lead magnet / entry point
- Main CTA

**3. Messaging Angles**
- Primary benefit claim
- Key proof points used
- Emotional vs. rational mix
- Fear-based vs. aspiration-based

**4. Creative Approach** (for ad analysis)
- Ad formats being run
- Visual style (lifestyle/product/testimonial)
- Hook types
- Offer in ad vs. landing page offer

**5. Social Proof**
- Type of proof used
- Specificity of results
- Volume indicators ("over X customers")
- Recency of proof

### Sources for Intelligence
- Website and landing pages
- Facebook Ad Library (for active ads)
- Google ads (search results for their keywords)
- Reviews (Google, Trustpilot, G2, App Store)
- LinkedIn (for B2B)
- Social media profiles
- Job listings (reveals priorities and tech stack)

## Analysis Output Format

```markdown
## Competitive Intelligence: [Market/Niche]
*Date: [Date]*
*Competitors analysed: [Number]*

### Market Landscape
[2-3 sentence summary of the competitive landscape]

### Competitor Profiles

#### [Competitor 1 Name]
**Positioning**: [Their core claim]
**Target audience**: [Who they're after]
**Key message**: [Primary message]
**Proof approach**: [How they back claims]
**Price signal**: [Premium/mid/value]
**Weakness**: [Gap or vulnerability]

[Repeat for each competitor]

### Gap Analysis
What nobody is saying:
- [Unaddressed pain point]
- [Unclaimed positioning territory]
- [Proof type nobody is using]

What everybody is saying (avoid these — table stakes):
- [Common claim 1]
- [Common claim 2]

### Opportunity Summary
**Best positioning gap**: [Specific recommendation]
**Best message angle**: [Specific recommendation]
**Best proof approach**: [Specific recommendation]
**Differentiation opportunity**: [How to stand out]
```

## Speed Version (15-Minute Analysis)
When time is limited:
1. Homepage headline from each competitor
2. Main CTA
3. Primary proof element
4. One-line summary of their positioning
5. One gap identified

## Integration Notes
- Feed insights into creative brief for ad campaigns
- Use gap analysis to inform positioning decisions
- Competitive messaging angles to avoid (to differentiate)
- Use as input for persona-architect (what are competitors NOT delivering?)
