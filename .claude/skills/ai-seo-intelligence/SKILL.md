# AI SEO Intelligence Skill

## Activation
Use this skill when: Creating SEO-optimised content, keyword strategy, on-page SEO, content that ranks AND converts.

**Activate with**: "Use AI SEO Intelligence", "Create SEO content", "Optimise for search"

## Core Philosophy
SEO in 2024+ is about satisfying search intent so completely that Google has no reason to rank anyone else. Every piece of SEO content must:
1. Match search intent perfectly
2. Answer the question better than any competitor
3. Demonstrate genuine expertise (E-E-A-T)
4. Convert visitors into leads/customers

## The SEO Content Hierarchy

### Tier 1: Pillar Pages (2,500-5,000 words)
- Comprehensive coverage of a broad topic
- Internal link hub for cluster content
- Targets head keywords (high volume, competitive)
- Goal: Establish topical authority

### Tier 2: Cluster Content (1,200-2,500 words)
- Specific subtopics of the pillar
- Links back to pillar page
- Targets long-tail keywords
- Goal: Cover all facets of the topic

### Tier 3: Supporting Content (600-1,200 words)
- Answers very specific questions
- Optimised for featured snippets
- Quick, direct, comprehensive answers
- Goal: Capture query-specific traffic

## Keyword Research Framework

### Intent Classification
**Informational** (Know): "What is...", "How to...", "Why does..."
→ Blog posts, guides, how-tos

**Navigational** (Go): Brand + product searches
→ Home/landing pages, brand content

**Commercial** (Do research): "Best...", "[X] vs [Y]", "[X] review"
→ Comparison pages, listicles, review content

**Transactional** (Do buy): "Buy...", "[X] price", "[X] near me"
→ Product/service pages, landing pages

### Keyword Selection Criteria
```
Primary keyword:
- Monthly search volume: 500+ (or 100+ for niche B2B)
- Keyword difficulty: Match to domain authority
- Intent: Matches page goal
- Commercial value: Can convert to revenue

Secondary keywords (3-5):
- Semantic variations
- Related questions
- Modifier variations
```

## Content Structure Framework

### The SEO Content Blueprint

```
Title Tag: [Primary Keyword] — [Benefit/Modifier] | [Brand]
(50-60 characters)

Meta Description: [Action verb] + [Primary benefit] + [Secondary benefit] + [CTA]
(150-160 characters)

H1: [Slight variation of title tag — more natural]

Introduction (150-200 words):
- Hook: Surprising stat, bold claim, or relatable problem
- Empathy: Acknowledge the challenge
- Promise: What they'll learn/gain
- Preview: What's covered in the article

Table of Contents (for 1,500+ word articles)

Body Sections:
H2: [Keyword-informed section title]
[300-500 words per major section]
  H3: [Subsection title]
  [100-200 words per subsection]

Conclusion (100-150 words):
- Summary of key points
- CTA (primary conversion goal)
```

### Featured Snippet Optimisation

**Paragraph snippet** (40-60 words):
Start with: "[Keyword] is [definition/answer]."
Follow with 2-3 supporting sentences.
End with relevance to the searcher.

**List snippet**:
H2: [Question as heading]
Use numbered or bulleted list
Keep each item to 1-2 sentences
5-8 items optimal

**Table snippet**:
Use actual HTML/Markdown table
Clear column headers
Data Google can lift directly

## E-E-A-T Enhancement

### Experience Signals
- First-person accounts of actually doing the thing
- Specific details only someone with experience would know
- Photos/screenshots of real work (note: text description for AI content)
- Dates and timeframes of when experience occurred

### Expertise Signals
- Author bio with relevant credentials
- Citing primary research and data
- Technical accuracy in all claims
- Going beyond surface-level information

### Authoritativeness Signals
- Links to and from authoritative sources
- Brand mentions across the web
- Social proof and testimonials from recognisable sources
- Industry awards or recognition

### Trustworthiness Signals
- Accurate, up-to-date information
- Transparent about limitations
- Clear contact information
- Privacy policy and terms
- SSL/security signals

## Content Optimisation Checklist

### Pre-Write
- [ ] Primary keyword identified and intent confirmed
- [ ] Top 5 ranking pages analysed for structure
- [ ] Questions from "People Also Ask" noted
- [ ] Related searches identified
- [ ] Competitor word count benchmarked
- [ ] Unique angle identified (what will we do better?)

### During Write
- [ ] Primary keyword in title, H1, first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword 3-5x throughout (natural density)
- [ ] Secondary keywords used naturally
- [ ] Headers use keyword variations
- [ ] Internal links to relevant pages (3-5 minimum)
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources (2-3 minimum)
- [ ] Images with keyword-relevant alt text
- [ ] FAQ section for long-tail question coverage

### Post-Write
- [ ] Title tag 50-60 characters
- [ ] Meta description 150-160 characters with CTA
- [ ] URL is short, keyword-rich, readable
- [ ] Schema markup planned/implemented
- [ ] Content answers the primary intent completely
- [ ] Content is longer/better than top 3 competitors

## Local SEO Module

### For Location-Based Clients
```
Page structure for local:
H1: [Service] in [City] — [Benefit]
First paragraph: Mention city/suburb 2-3 times naturally

Include:
- Specific local references (suburbs, landmarks, local context)
- Local schema markup
- NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone)
- Local testimonials and case studies
- Google Business Profile alignment
```

### Local Keyword Modifiers
- [Service] + [City]
- [Service] + [Suburb]
- [Service] + "near me"
- Best [service] + [City]
- [Service] + [City] + [qualifier]

## Content Types & When To Use

### Ultimate Guide
**When**: Pillar content, broad topic, want to be the definitive resource
**Structure**: 8-15 major sections, 3,000+ words, comprehensive coverage
**SEO value**: Topical authority, linkable asset, internal link hub

### How-To Article
**When**: Instructional intent, step-by-step process
**Structure**: Numbered steps, clear outcomes, prerequisite section
**SEO value**: Featured snippet target, clear intent match

### Comparison Post
**When**: Commercial investigation intent, "X vs Y" queries
**Structure**: Intro → both options → head-to-head comparison → verdict
**SEO value**: Captures buyers in research phase

### Listicle
**When**: "Best X for Y" or "Top X ways to" queries
**Structure**: Quick intro → numbered/bulleted list with detail → conclusion
**SEO value**: High CTR, clear structure, shareable

### Case Study
**When**: Showcasing results, building credibility, B2B content
**Structure**: Challenge → solution → results → key learnings
**SEO value**: Unique data, linkable, builds E-E-A-T

## Content Calendar Integration

### Monthly SEO Content Plan
```
Week 1: Pillar/cornerstone content (high effort, high value)
Week 2: Cluster content #1 (supporting pillar)
Week 3: Cluster content #2 + quick wins (FAQ, local pages)
Week 4: Performance content (refresh existing, conversion-focused)
```

### Content Refresh Protocol
Refresh content when:
- Rankings dropped more than 5 positions
- Content is 12+ months old
- Significant industry changes occurred
- Click-through rate below 2%

Refresh process:
1. Update statistics and examples
2. Add new sections covering gaps
3. Optimise for new related keywords
4. Improve internal linking
5. Update publish date

## SEO Copywriting Formulas

### Title Tag Formulas
- [Number] [Adjective] [Keyword] for [Year]
- How to [Achieve Result] with [Method/Tool]
- [Keyword]: The Complete Guide to [Benefit]
- [Primary Benefit] with [Solution Type] | [Brand]
- Why [Target Audience] [Achieve/Use] [Keyword]

### Meta Description Formulas
- "Discover [benefit] with our [content type]. [Specific proof/claim]. [CTA] →"
- "Learn [topic] from [authority credential]. [Key benefit]. [Urgency/offer]. [CTA] →"
- "[Question they're searching]? Here's [what they'll get]. [Proof element]. [CTA] →"

### H1 Formulas
- The [Adjective] Guide to [Keyword]: [Benefit]
- [Number] Ways to [Achieve Result] with [Keyword]
- How [Target Audience] Can [Achieve Goal] with [Keyword]

### Introduction Hook Formulas
- Stat hook: "[X]% of [group] [problem]. If you're one of them..."
- Question hook: "What if [desired outcome] was simpler than you thought?"
- Problem hook: "[Relatable problem]. It doesn't have to be this way."
- Counter hook: "Most guides about [keyword] miss the most important part."

## Measuring SEO Success

### 30-Day Metrics (Indexing Phase)
- Pages indexed: Check Search Console
- Initial ranking positions: Establish baseline
- Technical issues: No crawl errors

### 90-Day Metrics (Ranking Phase)
- Position improvements: Target top 20 for primary keyword
- Impression growth: 20%+ month-over-month
- CTR: Above 2% for branded, above 1% for non-branded

### 180-Day Metrics (Traffic Phase)
- Organic traffic: Target 50%+ growth
- Ranking targets: Top 10 for primary keywords
- Conversion rate: Track goal completions
- Revenue attribution: Connect to business outcomes
