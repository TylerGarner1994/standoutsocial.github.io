# Expert Review Panel Skill

## Activation
Use this skill when: Quality-checking important content through multiple expert perspectives before delivery.

**Activate with**: "Run expert review panel", "Get the expert panel's feedback", "Multi-expert review"

## What This Skill Does
Simulates a panel of domain experts reviewing content and providing structured, role-specific feedback. Each reviewer brings a different lens to identify blind spots.

## The Panel Members

### 1. The Direct Response Copywriter (25+ years)
**Focus**: Conversion mechanics, persuasion psychology, response rate
**Asks**:
- Does this make me want to buy/act immediately?
- Is the headline compelling enough to stop the scroll?
- Is the offer clear and the CTA unambiguous?
- Does the proof stack actually build belief?
- Are there any conversion killers (friction, confusion, trust gaps)?

**Feedback format**: "As a direct response copywriter, I would [change/strengthen/cut]..."

### 2. The Brand Strategist
**Focus**: Positioning, differentiation, brand consistency
**Asks**:
- Does this clearly communicate a distinctive position?
- Could any competitor use this exact copy? (If yes, it's not differentiated)
- Does this build long-term brand equity or just push for immediate response?
- Is the brand voice consistent throughout?

**Feedback format**: "From a brand strategy perspective, [concern/opportunity]..."

### 3. The Target Customer (Sceptic)
**Focus**: Relevance, believability, emotional resonance
**Asks**:
- Is this speaking to my real pain or a surface-level problem?
- Do I believe these claims?
- Does this feel like it's for me specifically?
- What would make me click away / stop reading?
- What question isn't being answered?

**Feedback format**: "As your target customer reading this, I [feel/think/want]..."

### 4. The Performance Marketer
**Focus**: Platform fit, metrics, testability
**Asks**:
- Will this pass platform policy review?
- Is the hook strong enough for cold audiences?
- Are there clear variants to A/B test?
- Does the funnel flow make sense (ad → landing page)?
- Are there any technical conversion killers?

**Feedback format**: "From a performance marketing standpoint, [recommendation]..."

### 5. The Lawyer/Compliance Reviewer
**Focus**: Claims, legal exposure, platform compliance
**Asks**:
- Are there claims that need substantiation?
- Are there income/health/financial claims that need disclaimers?
- Does this create unrealistic expectations?
- Are testimonials properly attributed and compliant?

**Feedback format**: "Flag for compliance: [specific concern + recommended fix]..."

## Review Process

### Step 1: Content Submission
Receive the content to review. Confirm:
- Content type (ad, landing page, email, etc.)
- Platform it's for
- Target audience
- Conversion goal

### Step 2: Panel Review
Each panel member reviews the content through their specific lens and provides:
- Top 3 strengths
- Top 3 concerns/weaknesses
- Specific recommendations (with rewrites where relevant)
- Overall verdict (Approve / Approve With Changes / Revise)

### Step 3: Synthesis
Synthesize the panel feedback into:
- Priority changes (must fix before publishing)
- Recommended changes (would significantly improve performance)
- Optional improvements (nice-to-have refinements)
- Approved elements (keep exactly as is)

## Quick Review (5 Minutes)
For rapid feedback:
1. Copywriter: Does this make me want to act? What's the #1 issue?
2. Customer: Is this speaking to my real pain? What's missing?
3. Compliance: Any red flags I need to note?
4. Verdict: Approve / Change this / Start over

## Integration Notes
- Run this BEFORE sending anything to client
- Run this BEFORE launching any paid traffic
- Use after every major content piece
- Can be run on competitor content to identify weaknesses
